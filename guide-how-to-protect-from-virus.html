01 — Threat Landscape

Where infections actually come from

  • Pirated software / cracked installers / key generators
  • Phishing emails and spoofed sender accounts
  • Malicious or lookalike URLs (credential harvesting)
  • Infected USB drives and unknown external devices
  • Unpatched OS, browser, or app vulnerabilities
Reality check: most infections are a human decision (click, download, plug-in), not a hacker breaking through firewalls.

Verify Protection

↓ to dive in
02 — Verify Protection

Check Defender status

Get-MpComputerStatus | Select-Object RealTimeProtectionEnabled, AntivirusEnabled,
  AntivirusSignatureLastUpdated, FullScanAge, QuickScanAge
Good state: RealTimeProtectionEnabled = True, signatures updated within last day.
02 — Verify Protection

Check Firewall status

Get-NetFirewallProfile | Select-Object Name, Enabled,
  DefaultInboundAction, DefaultOutboundAction

Check all 3 profiles: Domain, Private, Public — all should show Enabled = True.

Note: "NotConfigured" on inbound/outbound action is normal — means Windows secure defaults apply.

Section 4 — Run a Full Scan

04

Run a Full Scan

Step-by-step: launch, monitor, confirm clean.

↓ to dive in
04 — Run a Full Scan

Workflow

1
Check current scan age first
Get-MpComputerStatus → FullScanAge
2
Launch full scan (runs in background)
Start-MpScan -ScanType FullScan
3
Let it run — can take 30min to 2hrs
Scans every file on disk, non-destructive, read-only
4
Confirm completion + check threats
Get-MpComputerStatus → FullScanAge = 0
5
List any detections found
Get-MpThreatDetection
04 — Run a Full Scan

Reading the result

Clean: Get-MpThreatDetection returns empty output — nothing found.
Threat found: Get-MpThreatDetection lists ThreatID + Resources + InitialDetectionTime — Defender typically auto-quarantines, verify via Windows Security app.

Run full scan monthly. Quick scan (daily, automatic) covers common infection points only.

Section 5 — Limits & Tips

05

Limits & Tips

What antivirus can't do, and cheap habits that cover the gap.

↓ to dive in
05 — Limits & Tips
LimitWhy it matters
No AV catches 100% of new threatsSignature-based detection lags brand-new malware
Can't stop you from typing password into phishing siteSocial engineering bypasses technical controls entirely
Can't undo ransomware encryptionOnly backups restore files — AV just blocks/detects
05 — Limits & Tips

Habits that matter more than any tool

  • Never download cracked software or key generators
  • Hover/check URLs before entering credentials anywhere
  • Treat unexpected attachments as hostile, even from known contacts
  • Use MFA app (not SMS) on email, banking, cloud storage
  • Keep 3-2-1 backup: 3 copies, 2 media types, 1 offsite/cloud
